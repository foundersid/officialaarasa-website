Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly is carob?

Carob is made from pods of the carob tree (Ceratonia siliqua), native to the Mediterranean. The pods are dried, roasted, and ground into a naturally sweet powder. It's been consumed for thousands of years.

Does carob taste like chocolate?

No. Carob has its own unique flavor - earthy, naturally sweet, and mildly nutty. While similar in appearance to cocoa, it's distinctly different in taste. We encourage you to enjoy it as its own superfood, not as a chocolate substitute.

Is carob safe during pregnancy?

Yes! Carob is caffeine-free and generally safe during pregnancy. However, we always recommend consulting your doctor before adding any new food to your diet, especially if you're pregnant or nursing.

Can children consume carob?

Yes, carob is safe for children above 3 years. It's caffeine-free, making it a great alternative to chocolate or cocoa for kids' drinks and treats.

Why is the texture grainy?

Carob is naturally high in fiber (37.9g per 100g). The slight graininess is a sign of pure, minimally processed carob. For smoother texture, blend well or use a frother.

How much should I consume daily?

Start with 1 tablespoon (7g) daily and gradually increase to 2 tablespoons. Due to high fiber content, increase intake slowly and drink plenty of water.

What's the shelf life?

12 months from the manufacturing date when sealed. Once opened, best consumed within 3 months. Store in a cool, dry place in an airtight container.

Is it organic?

Our carob is sourced from premium farms in Spain. While not certified organic, it's tested to be free from harmful pesticides and contaminants. Lab certificate available for download.

Can diabetics consume carob?

Carob contains natural sugars (33.4g per 100g from the carob pods themselves). While it's lower glycemic than refined sugar, diabetics should consult their doctor and monitor blood sugar levels.
